Why your IELTS score matters more than you think

For applicants applying directly as permanent residents, the IELTS exam is a make or break. Here's why — and what scores you should be aiming for.

5 min read · MVC Editorial Team

For people interested in immigrating to Canada directly as permanent residents (PR), the International English Language Testing System (IELTS) exam is a make or break. The Canadian government prioritizes candidates with a strong command of English for an obvious reason: Canada is predominantly an English-speaking country, and applicants with high IELTS scores are more likely to assimilate into Canadian society quickly.

Why language scores carry so much weight

Canada has slow population growth, and as the second-largest country in the world, it needs immigrants to keep its economy strong over the long term. For that reason it remains open to newcomers — but it has set clear standards for who's accepted directly as a permanent resident.

Beyond English proficiency (measured through IELTS or, for French, TEF/TCF), age, skilled work experience, education level, and family ties in Canada are heavily weighted. Applicants with the right combination of all of these are typically granted permanent residence within 6 to 8 months of submission.

What scores should you be aiming for?

Below are the four categories and the brackets we use as rules of thumb when assessing eligibility for direct PR pathways. (Note: the official cut-offs vary by program and by draw — these are practical targets, not regulatory minimums.)

Listening

  • 8.5–9.0 — Ideal
  • 7.5–8.0 — Average
  • 6.0–7.0 — Below average

Reading

  • 8.0–9.0 — Ideal
  • 7.0–7.5 — Average
  • Below 7.0 — Below average

Speaking

  • 7.5–9.0 — Ideal
  • 7.0 — Average
  • Below 7.0 — Below average

Writing

  • 7.5–9.0 — Ideal
  • 7.0 — Average
  • Below 7.0 — Below average
If you're 31 or older at the time of application, your IELTS scores need to be in the "ideal" range to maximize your chances. Age points get tighter every year you wait.

Why age changes the math

If you're excited about the quality of life in Canada and want to apply directly as a permanent resident, don't take the IELTS exam for granted — especially if you're in your thirties.

The Canadian government weights several factors. If you're 31 or older at the time of application, your IELTS scores should be in the "ideal" range to maximize your chances of success. If you're 30 or younger and have IELTS scores in the "average" range, you still have a real chance of being accepted directly as a PR — simply because you're younger.

What if your scores aren't there yet?

There are other immigration streams available if your English level isn't ideal, you're past 31, you have less education, less skilled work experience, or no family ties in Canada. The right path depends on your specific profile — a Provincial Nominee stream, a study-to-PR pathway, or a work permit that builds toward Canadian experience can all be strong alternatives.

RCIC honest take

One IELTS retake is often the highest-impact thing you can do for your CRS score. We routinely see clients gain 30–80 points by retaking with focused preparation. If your scores are in the "average" range, it's almost always worth one more attempt before submitting.
