Express Entry Stream

Federal Skilled Trades Program (FSTP)

The Express Entry stream for tradespeople — electricians, welders, chefs, mechanics, plumbers, machinists, heavy-equipment operators, and more.

Book a Free Consultation

What is the Federal Skilled Trades Program?

The Federal Skilled Trades Program is the Express Entry stream built specifically for journeypersons and skilled tradespeople. Where the Federal Skilled Worker Program rewards education and language, FSTP rewards hands-on qualification: years on the tools, a certificate of qualification, and an employer who values you enough to put a job offer in writing.

Canada has a structural shortage of skilled trades, and FSTP reflects that. Language requirements are lower (CLB 5 speaking/listening, CLB 4 reading/writing). Education is not required. The bar is real-world qualification — and that's where most foreign-trained tradespeople hit a wall.

FSTP requires either a Canadian provincial or territorial certificate of qualification in your trade — typically earned by passing the Red Seal exam — or a valid full-time job offer of at least 1 year from a Canadian employer. Our work is usually making one of those two paths real.

Key Facts

Trades Work Experience 2 years (3,120 hours) in the last 5 years
Eligible Occupations NOC Major Groups 72 (exc. 726), 73, 82, 83, 92, 93 (exc. 932)
Language CLB 5 speaking/listening · CLB 4 reading/writing
Education Not required
Required Credential Certificate of qualification or 1-year job offer
Settlement Funds Required (waived with valid job offer)
Province Outside Quebec

Eligibility

Do you qualify?

FSTP has four core requirements — meet all of them and you're eligible to enter the Express Entry pool.

  • 2 years (3,120 hours) of paid full-time skilled trades work experience in the last 5 years — Canada or abroad — after you became qualified in your trade
  • Work experience in an eligible NOC trade — Major Groups 72 (excluding 726), 73, 82, 83, 92, or 93 (excluding 932)
  • Either a Canadian certificate of qualification in your trade, OR a valid full-time job offer of at least 1 year from a Canadian employer
  • A language test result of CLB 5 in speaking and listening, and CLB 4 in reading and writing — in English or French
  • Settlement funds appropriate for your family size — waived if you have a valid job offer and authorization to work in Canada
  • You plan to live outside Quebec and are not inadmissible

A certificate of qualification almost always means writing the Red Seal exam (or your province's equivalent). If your trade is not regulated in your destination province, you must use the job-offer route. Book a free FSTP eligibility check — we'll map your trade to the right path.

The Process

How it works

From foreign-trained tradesperson to Canadian permanent resident.

  1. 1

    Trade & eligibility check

    We map your trade to the correct NOC code, confirm your hours, and identify whether the certificate-of-qualification route or the job-offer route is realistic for you.

  2. 2

    Certificate of qualification or job offer

    For the certificate route, we coordinate with the provincial trade authority and prep you for the Red Seal exam. For the job-offer route, we work with the employer on the LMIA or LMIA-exempt offer.

  3. 3

    Language test

    FSTP language requirements are lower than other streams (CLB 5 speaking/listening, CLB 4 reading/writing). We help you choose between IELTS, CELPIP, TEF, or TCF and prepare strategically.

  4. 4

    Express Entry profile & ITA

    We submit your Express Entry profile under FSTP. Trades-category draws often clear at lower CRS than general draws — your odds may be better than they look.

  5. 5

    PR application & biometrics

    On ITA, we file the full PR application within the 60-day window — reference letters, employer documentation, language results, medical, biometrics, and personal history.

  6. 6

    COPR & landing

    Once approved, you receive your Confirmation of Permanent Residence and (if required) a PR visa, then complete landing in Canada.

Frequently Asked

FSTP FAQs

Which trades qualify for the Federal Skilled Trades Program?

FSTP eligible occupations are in NOC Major Groups 72 (excluding Sub-Major Group 726), 73, 82, 83, 92, and 93 (excluding Sub-Major Group 932). This covers most industrial, electrical, construction, maintenance, transportation, agricultural, processing, and manufacturing trades — plus chefs, cooks, butchers, and bakers.

What language level do I need for FSTP?

FSTP language requirements are lower than other Express Entry streams. You need CLB 5 in speaking and listening, and CLB 4 in reading and writing — in either English or French. This recognizes that trades work depends more on hands-on communication than written reports.

Do I need a Canadian certificate of qualification, or will my foreign training do?

Foreign training alone is not enough. You need either a certificate of qualification from a Canadian provincial or territorial trade authority (which usually requires passing the Red Seal exam or equivalent) OR a valid full-time job offer of at least 1 year from a Canadian employer in your trade.

How much skilled trades work experience do I need?

You need at least 2 years of full-time skilled trades work experience (or an equivalent amount in part-time work) within the 5 years before you apply — that's 3,120 hours total. The work must be paid and after you became qualified to work in your trade.

My province doesn't issue certificates in my trade — what then?

If your destination province doesn't regulate your trade, your only FSTP route is a valid 1-year job offer from a Canadian employer. We can also assess whether a different province (where the trade is regulated) is feasible, or whether the Federal Skilled Worker Program is a better fit.
