Express Entry Stream

Canadian Experience Class (CEC)

The fastest Express Entry route for skilled workers who are already contributing to Canada's economy — typically processed in about six months once invited.

Book a Free Consultation

What is the Canadian Experience Class?

The Canadian Experience Class is one of three federal economic streams managed under Express Entry. It is designed for people who have already worked in a skilled occupation in Canada and want to transition to permanent residence. If you came to Canada as a graduate, on a work permit, or under an LMIA, CEC is usually the most direct path.

Because applicants have lived and worked in Canada, the federal government considers them lower-risk: language proficiency is established, Canadian credentials and work history are documented, and integration is already underway. As a result, CEC has no proof-of-funds requirement, and processing once invited typically targets six months.

That said, CEC is competitive. The Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) score that earns an Invitation to Apply (ITA) moves with each draw, and small details — language test scores, the timing of your work history, spouse points — can be the difference between an invitation and another six months in the pool.

Key Facts

Skilled Work Experience 1 year (1,560 hours) in Canada, within the last 3 years
Eligible Occupations NOC TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3
Language (TEER 0 or 1) CLB 7 in all four abilities
Language (TEER 2 or 3) CLB 5 in all four abilities
Education Not required (but adds CRS points)
Proof of Funds Not required
Processing After ITA ~6 months target

Eligibility

Do you qualify?

To be eligible for the Canadian Experience Class, you must meet each of the following.

  • At least 1 year (1,560 hours) of skilled work experience in Canada in the 3 years before you apply — full-time, part-time equivalent, or combined
  • Your work was in a NOC TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3 occupation — managerial, professional, technical, or skilled trades
  • You were legally authorized to work in Canada throughout the experience (e.g. PGWP, employer-specific work permit, IEC)
  • An approved language test result of CLB 7 (TEER 0 or 1) or CLB 5 (TEER 2 or 3) in English or French — speaking, listening, reading, writing
  • You plan to live outside Quebec (Quebec selects skilled workers under its own programs)
  • You are not inadmissible on criminal, medical, or security grounds

What does not count: self-employment, work performed while you were a full-time student (including co-op terms), and any work done without authorization. Book a free CEC eligibility check — we'll confirm your hours and TEER classification before you spend a dollar on a language test.

The Process

How it works

From eligibility check to permanent residence — typically 8–12 months end-to-end if your CRS is competitive.

  1. 1

    CEC eligibility & CRS forecast

    We confirm your hours, TEER classification, and language pathway, then forecast your CRS score against recent draws so you know what you're aiming for.

  2. 2

    Language test & ECA (if needed)

    We help you choose between IELTS / CELPIP / TEF / TCF and book strategically. If a foreign degree boosts your CRS, we coordinate the Educational Credential Assessment.

  3. 3

    Express Entry profile

    We create your Express Entry profile, optimize every CRS-affecting detail (work history, education, spouse factors, French bonus), and submit it.

  4. 4

    Invitation to Apply (ITA)

    When your score clears the cut-off, IRCC issues an ITA. You then have 60 days to file the full PR application.

  5. 5

    PR application & biometrics

    We compile your full eAPR — reference letters, work permits, language results, medical, biometrics, and personal history — and submit within the 60-day window.

  6. 6

    COPR & landing

    Once approved, you receive your Confirmation of Permanent Residence and become a permanent resident. We brief you on landing logistics and what to expect at IRCC.

Frequently Asked

CEC FAQs

Does my Canadian work experience count if I worked while I was a student?

No. Work experience gained while you were a full-time student — including paid co-op work terms — does not count toward the CEC minimum. Only skilled work performed after you graduated, on a valid post-graduation work permit or other work authorization, qualifies.

I have 18 months of skilled work in Canada — do I qualify?

Likely yes, provided that experience was earned in a NOC TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3 occupation while you were authorized to work, your language test meets CLB 7 (for TEER 0 or 1) or CLB 5 (for TEER 2 or 3), and the experience was within the three years immediately before you apply.

Do I need proof of funds for the Canadian Experience Class?

No. CEC applicants are exempt from the settlement funds requirement that applies to the Federal Skilled Worker Program and Federal Skilled Trades Program. You do not need to show bank statements as part of your CEC PR application.

Does self-employment in Canada count toward CEC?

No. CEC requires paid work for an employer while you held valid Canadian work authorization. Self-employment income, contract work without an employer relationship, and unpaid internships do not count toward the one-year minimum.

My CRS score is below the recent cut-off — am I out?

Not necessarily. CRS cut-offs move every draw, and category-based draws (e.g. French-language proficiency, healthcare, trades) can lower the threshold for qualifying candidates.
