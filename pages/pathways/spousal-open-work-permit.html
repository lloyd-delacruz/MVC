Spousal Open Work Permit

Move to Canada together. While one of you studies or works post-graduation, your partner can live here, work freely, and start building their own path to permanent residence.

What is a Spousal Open Work Permit?

For couples that are married or in a common-law relationship, the Spousal Open Work Permit allows the spouse of an international student — or of a PGWP holder working in a skilled position — to come to Canada and work for almost any employer, full-time or part-time, anywhere in the country.

"Open" is the key word. Your partner is not tied to a specific employer, role, or province. They can change jobs freely, work in any sector, and start accumulating Canadian work experience that strengthens the family's case for permanent residence later on.

For many couples, this permit is what makes the move to Canada possible — financially, emotionally, and practically. One person's study journey becomes two careers, two incomes, and a shared route to PR.

Key Facts

Who It's For Spouse or common-law partner of an eligible student or PGWP holder
Permit Type Open — almost any employer, almost any role
Permit Length Matched to the principal's study or work permit
Hours Full-time, part-time — no employer hour cap
Leads To Canadian work experience for joint PR planning

Eligibility

Does your spouse qualify?

Eligibility depends on what you are doing in Canada — studying, or working under a PGWP. Either route opens the door for your partner.

If you are studying full-time

  • You hold a valid study permit
  • You are a full-time student
  • You are studying at a Designated Learning Institution that offers PGWP-eligible programs

If you are working under a PGWP

  • You hold a valid work permit
  • You are working in a skilled position (TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3)
  • Your work permit has at least 6 months remaining when your spouse applies

Rules around eligible programs and skill levels change. We confirm both partners' status before filing. Send us your details for a quick assessment.

The Process

How it works

We file together — your study or work file, and your spouse's open work permit — so the family arrives ready to live and earn from day one.

  1. 1

    Confirm relationship status

    We document the marriage or common-law relationship — certificates, cohabitation evidence, joint accounts, photos, communications. Strong relationship evidence is the most common refusal point.

  2. 2

    Verify principal applicant eligibility

    We confirm that you (the student or worker) hold the right kind of permit in the right kind of program or skilled position — the foundation that makes the spousal permit possible.

  3. 3

    Prepare both applications together

    We build the spousal open work permit file alongside your study or PGWP file, so the dates, evidence, and storyline match cleanly across both applications.

  4. 4

    Submit to IRCC

    Filed online with full documentation, fees, and (where required) biometrics scheduled at a local Visa Application Centre.

  5. 5

    Arrive in Canada together

    Once approved, both permits are issued at the port of entry. Your spouse can begin working immediately — no LMIA, no employer to find first.

  6. 6

    Plan PR as a family

    Both partners' Canadian work experience can count toward future PR applications. We map the strongest joint pathway — often Express Entry with the higher-scoring partner as principal applicant.
