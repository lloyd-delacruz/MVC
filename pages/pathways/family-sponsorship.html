Family Sponsorship

Reunite with your spouse, partner, children, or parents in Canada — we handle every step of the process.

Book a Free Consultation

What is Family Sponsorship?

Canada's family class sponsorship lets a Canadian citizen or permanent resident sponsor close family members to become permanent residents. The most common categories are spouses, common-law and conjugal partners, dependent children, and parents and grandparents (under the Parents and Grandparents Program, with an annual intake cap).

Sponsorship comes with a legal commitment called the undertaking. By signing it, you agree to provide for your sponsored family member's basic needs and to repay any social assistance they receive during the undertaking period — 3 years for spouses and partners, up to 20 years for parents and grandparents.

The biggest risks in sponsorship applications are not eligibility but presentation: relationship genuineness, document completeness, and financial proof. We help you build a file that anticipates IRCC's questions before they're asked.

Key Facts

Processing Time 12–24 months (varies by category)
Who It's For Spouses, partners, children, parents, grandparents
Language Requirement None for sponsor; none for sponsored person
Key Eligibility Factor Genuine relationship, sponsor financial capacity
Consultant Value Medium — file presentation is decisive

Eligibility

Do you qualify?

Sponsors and sponsored persons each need to meet specific requirements. Here are the most common.

  • Sponsor is a Canadian citizen or permanent resident aged 18 or older
  • Sponsor meets the Minimum Necessary Income (MNI) threshold for parents/grandparents (no income test for spouses or dependent children)
  • Sponsor is not bankrupt, not in default on a previous undertaking, and not in receipt of social assistance (except for disability)
  • The relationship is genuine and not entered primarily for immigration purposes
  • Sponsored person is not inadmissible on criminal, medical, or security grounds

The Process

How it works

From confirming eligibility to your family member's arrival in Canada.

  1. 1

    Confirm sponsor and sponsored eligibility

    We review the sponsor's status, income (where applicable), and the relationship category to confirm both parties qualify before any forms are filed.

  2. 2

    Gather documents

    Relationship evidence, financial records, ID and civil documents, photos, and affidavits. We give you a precise list and review every document for completeness.

  3. 3

    Submit the sponsorship application

    The sponsor and sponsored person applications are filed together to IRCC, with the undertaking signed and supporting evidence attached.

  4. 4

    Relationship assessment

    IRCC reviews the genuineness of the relationship. Additional evidence — or rarely an interview — may be requested.

  5. 5

    Medical & biometrics

    The sponsored person completes a medical exam with an IRCC-approved physician and provides biometrics at a Visa Application Centre.

  6. 6

    Decision & arrival

    Once approved, the sponsored person receives their Confirmation of Permanent Residence and (if required) a PR Visa, then completes landing in Canada.
