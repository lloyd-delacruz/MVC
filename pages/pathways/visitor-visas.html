What is a Visitor Visa?

A Temporary Resident Visa (TRV) — commonly called a visitor visa or tourist visa — is the document most foreign nationals need to enter Canada for short stays. Whether you need one depends primarily on your passport. Citizens of visa-required countries need a TRV; citizens of visa-exempt countries travelling by air need an Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA) instead.

TRVs come in single-entry and multiple-entry forms. Multiple-entry visas are issued by default in most cases and let you visit Canada repeatedly during the visa's validity, typically for up to 6 months per visit. Visitors already in Canada can also apply to extend their stay through a visitor record.

The most common refusal reasons are weak ties to the home country, insufficient funds, and unclear purpose of visit. A well-organized application that addresses these concerns directly — with the right supporting letters — significantly improves your odds.

Key Facts

Processing Time 2–8 weeks (varies by country)
Who It's For Nationals of non-eTA-eligible countries
Language Requirement None
Key Eligibility Factor Strong ties to home country
Consultant Value Low–Medium — higher after a refusal

Eligibility

Do you qualify?

IRCC's job is to confirm you'll come, visit as planned, and leave on time. These are the elements they assess.

  • A valid passport covering the entire duration of your intended stay
  • Proof of funds sufficient for your visit and return travel
  • Demonstrated strong ties to your home country — employment, property, family, or other reasons to return
  • No criminal or medical inadmissibility to Canada
  • A genuine visitor intent — you're not coming to work, study, or stay permanently
  • A clearly documented purpose of visit — itinerary, invitation letter, or business agenda

The Process

How it works

Most visitor visa applications are decided in a few weeks.

  1. 1

    Confirm a TRV is required

    We confirm whether your nationality requires a TRV or whether an eTA is sufficient — this depends on your passport, not your residence.

  2. 2

    Gather documents

    Passport, photos, financial documents, employment proof, ties to home country, invitation letter (if applicable), and a clear travel itinerary.

  3. 3

    Submit online application

    Filed through the IRCC portal with the right supporting letters to address the most common refusal grounds before they come up.

  4. 4

    Biometrics (if required)

    Many visitor visa applicants are required to provide biometrics at a Visa Application Centre. Biometrics are typically valid for 10 years.

  5. 5

    Decision

    IRCC decides. If approved, your passport is stamped with the TRV and you can travel to Canada. The actual length of stay is determined by the officer at the port of entry — usually up to 6 months.
