What is a Canadian Work Permit?

A Canadian work permit lets a foreign national work for a specific employer or (in some cases) any employer in Canada. There are two broad categories. Closed work permits are tied to a single named employer, role, and location. Open work permits let you work for almost any employer anywhere in Canada and are reserved for specific situations like spouses of skilled workers and post-graduation graduates.

Closed permits typically require a Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) — a document from Employment and Social Development Canada confirming that hiring a foreign worker won't displace a Canadian. But many work permits are LMIA-exempt through international agreements and government policies: the International Experience Canada (IEC) youth mobility program, CUSMA (formerly NAFTA) for U.S. and Mexican professionals, the Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) for international graduates, and intra-company transfers.

Choosing the right category — and getting the documentation right — is the difference between a 4-week approval and months of processing or a refusal.

Key Facts

Processing Time 2–16 weeks (varies by category)
Who It's For Foreign workers with a job offer or eligible category
Language Requirement Varies by program (often none)
Key Eligibility Factor Job offer or LMIA-exempt category
Consultant Value Medium — category selection is critical

Eligibility

Do you qualify?

Specifics vary widely by category. These are the common requirements across most work permits.

  • A valid job offer from a Canadian employer or eligibility under an LMIA-exempt category
  • No criminal or medical inadmissibility to Canada
  • Sufficient funds to support yourself (and any accompanying family) during your stay
  • Demonstrated intent to leave at the end of your authorized stay, or clear eligibility to extend or transition to PR
  • You meet the occupation requirements — qualifications, licensing, language for the role

The Process

How it works

From identifying the right permit type to receiving your decision.

  1. 1

    Confirm your permit type

    We assess your situation against the LMIA-based, LMIA-exempt, and open work permit categories to find the fastest, lowest-risk option.

  2. 2

    Gather documents

    Job offer letter, employer documents, qualifications, proof of funds, police certificates, medical (if needed), and category-specific evidence.

  3. 3

    Employer obtains LMIA (if required)

    If your role requires an LMIA, your employer applies to ESDC. We coordinate with the employer to make sure their application is complete and aligned with yours.

  4. 4

    Submit work permit application

    We file your application online or, where required, via a Visa Application Centre. Most applicants apply from outside Canada; some can apply at the port of entry.

  5. 5

    Biometrics

    If required, you provide biometrics at a Visa Application Centre or Application Support Centre.

  6. 6

    Decision

    IRCC decides. Approved applicants receive a Port of Entry letter and pick up their work permit on arrival in Canada — or, if applying from inside Canada, are issued the permit directly.
