What's changed in BC PNP Entrepreneur Immigration

  • 50% federal allocation cut (Jan 2025). IRCC reduced B.C.'s 2025 PNP allocation from 8,000 to 4,000 nominations across all streams. Registration scoring and proposal quality matter more than ever.
  • 2026–2028 Immigration Levels Plan signals planned increases to PNP admissions over the plan period — but allocations remain below 2024 levels.
  • Look West strategy. BC PNP continues to issue High Economic Impact invitations across all sectors, prioritizing entrepreneurs and skilled workers who deliver measurable economic benefit.
  • Skills Immigration fee increase (Jan 22, 2026). Skills Immigration applications rose from $1,475 to $1,750. Entrepreneur Immigration fees are separate; we confirm current EI amounts in your consultation.

What is the BC PNP Entrepreneur Base Category?

The Base Category is British Columbia's main entrepreneur stream. It is designed for experienced business owners and senior managers who want to start a new business or buy and grow an existing one in B.C. — anywhere in the province, including Metro Vancouver.

Unlike federal investor programs, the Base Category is a two-step process: you arrive in B.C. on a 20-month work permit supported by the BC PNP, set up and actively manage your business, meet the performance milestones in your Performance Agreement, and only then receive your provincial nomination for permanent residence.

Selection is competitive. After you submit a registration, the BC PNP scores it on human capital factors (work and ownership experience, education, official-language ability) and economic factors (commercial viability, jobs created, transferability to B.C., and benefit to the province). Registrations are ranked and the top-scoring proposals receive Invitations to Apply (ITAs).

Key Facts

Minimum Personal Net Worth CAD $600,000 (verified by BC PNP-authorized accounting firm)
Minimum Business Investment CAD $200,000 in an eligible B.C. business
Job Creation At least 1 new full-time job for a Canadian citizen or PR
Active Management Required — you must run day-to-day operations
Eligible Locations Anywhere in B.C., including Metro Vancouver
Initial Status ~20-month work permit before nomination
Path to PR Provincial nomination → IRCC PR application

Eligibility

Do you qualify?

The Base Category has hard minimums plus a competitive scoring grid. You need both.

  • Personal net worth of at least CAD $600,000, legally accumulated and verifiable
  • A minimum eligible business investment of CAD $200,000 in your proposed B.C. business
  • Plan to create at least 1 new full-time job for a Canadian citizen or permanent resident
  • Demonstrated business ownership or senior management experience (typically 3+ years)
  • Active day-to-day management of the business — you must be on the ground in B.C., not a passive investor
  • Approved language test result in English or French (CLB 4 or higher recommended for points)
  • A commercially viable business proposal aligned with B.C.'s economy — that scores well on the BC PNP grid

Meeting the minimums is necessary but not sufficient. The Base Category is a competitive ranked draw — your proposal has to outscore other registrants to receive an ITA. Book a free pre-registration review — we'll forecast your likely score before you commit.

The Process

How it works

From business plan to permanent residence — typically 24–36 months end-to-end.

  1. 1

    Eligibility & scoring forecast

    We confirm your net worth, ownership/management experience, and business idea, then forecast your registration score against recent draws so you know whether you're competitive.

  2. 2

    Business proposal & registration

    We help you build a commercially viable business plan and complete your BC PNP registration. The business concept is scored separately by BC PNP staff within ~6 weeks.

  3. 3

    Qualified pool & Invitation to Apply (ITA)

    Eligible registrations sit in a qualified pool for up to 6 months. Top-scoring proposals receive ITAs at periodic draws.

  4. 4

    Application & net-worth verification

    After ITA, you submit a full application. A BC PNP-authorized accounting firm verifies your $600,000 net worth, legal source of funds, and accumulation history.

  5. 5

    Performance Agreement & work permit support

    On approval, you sign a Performance Agreement with the BC PNP and receive a work permit support letter (typically 20 months) so you can move to B.C. and execute the plan.

  6. 6

    Establish & operate the business

    You arrive in B.C., invest the $200,000, set up operations, hire your Canadian/PR employee(s), and actively manage day-to-day for a sustained period — typically at least 12 months of active management before final nomination.

  7. 7

    Final nomination & PR application

    Once you've met the Performance Agreement milestones, BC PNP issues your provincial nomination. You then submit your PR application to IRCC and complete medical, biometrics, and security checks.

  8. 8

    COPR & permanent residence

    IRCC issues your Confirmation of Permanent Residence and you become a Canadian permanent resident — with a real B.C. business already running.

Frequently Asked

Base Category FAQs

What is the minimum personal net worth?

The minimum verified personal net worth is CAD $600,000. After invitation, a BC PNP-authorized accounting firm reviews your net worth, source of funds, and accumulation history before your nomination application can proceed.

How much do I need to invest in my B.C. business?

The minimum eligible business investment is CAD $200,000. The investment must be in an eligible business that you will actively manage day-to-day, and you must create at least one new full-time job for a Canadian citizen or permanent resident.

Do I get permanent residence right away?

No. The Base Category nominates you only after you arrive in B.C. and successfully establish your business. You first receive a BC PNP work permit support letter for a roughly 20-month work permit, then operate the business and meet your Performance Agreement milestones before final nomination and PR submission.

Can the business be in Metro Vancouver?

Yes. Unlike the Regional Pilot, the Base Category permits businesses anywhere in British Columbia, including Metro Vancouver, Victoria, and other large metros.

Has anything changed recently?

Yes. In January 2025 the federal government cut British Columbia's annual PNP allocation from 8,000 to 4,000 — a 50% reduction. The 2026–2028 Immigration Levels Plan signals increased PNP admissions over time, but allocations remain tighter than 2024 levels, so registration scoring and proposal quality are more important than ever.

Should I choose Base Category or Regional Pilot?

The Regional Pilot has lower thresholds (net worth $300,000, investment $100,000) but restricts you to smaller communities outside Metro Vancouver and requires a community referral. Choose Base if you want metro flexibility and have the capital; choose Regional Pilot if your business fits a smaller community and your capital is between $300K and $600K.
