Post-Graduation Work Permit

Turn your Canadian degree into Canadian work experience — and one of the strongest pathways to permanent residence.

What is a Post-Graduation Work Permit?

A Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) is an open work permit that lets graduates of eligible Canadian programs stay and work in Canada after they finish their studies. It's open — meaning you can work for almost any employer, in almost any role, anywhere in Canada.

For most international students, the PGWP is the bridge that turns a study permit into permanent residence. Twelve months of skilled Canadian work experience opens the door to Express Entry's Canadian Experience Class — and provincial streams designed to retain graduates educated here.

The length of your PGWP depends on the length of the program you completed. Generally: 8–24 months of study yields a PGWP equal to your program length; a 2-year-or-longer program can yield a 3-year PGWP. We'll map this out for you precisely based on your DLI and program.

Key Facts

Permit Length Up to 3 years, based on program length
Work Type Open — most employers, most roles
One-Time Only A PGWP is granted once in a lifetime
Application Window Within 180 days of program completion
Leads To Express Entry · CEC · PNP graduate streams

Eligibility

Do you qualify?

The PGWP rewards completion of a real, full-time program at a recognised institution. The criteria are firm — but clear.

  • You are 18 years of age or older
  • You held a valid study permit for the duration of your studies
  • You graduated from a PGWP-eligible Designated Learning Institution
  • You completed a full-time program of at least 8 months in length
  • You apply within 180 days of receiving final confirmation that you completed the program

Unsure if your DLI or program qualifies? Send us your transcript — we'll confirm eligibility before you apply.

The Process

How it works

From program completion to a 3-year work permit in hand.

  1. 1

    Confirm program completion

    Wait for your DLI to issue a final transcript and a letter confirming that you have met all program requirements. This is the document that starts the 180-day clock.

  2. 2

    Verify PGWP eligibility

    Not every program at every institution qualifies. We confirm your DLI is on the PGWP-eligible list and that your program length, mode of delivery, and credential type all meet IRCC's requirements.

  3. 3

    Decide where to apply from

    You can apply from inside Canada (and start working immediately under implied status if your study permit is still valid) or from abroad. The right choice depends on travel plans, study permit expiry, and family.

  4. 4

    Submit the PGWP application

    Filed online to IRCC with your transcript, completion letter, passport, photos, and fees. We package the file so processing is clean and fast.

  5. 5

    Begin Canadian work experience

    Once approved, start building the Canadian work experience that opens Express Entry's Canadian Experience Class — typically after 12 months of full-time skilled work.

  6. 6

    Pivot to permanent residence

    We map your PR strategy from day one — CEC, PNP graduate streams, or a province-specific pathway — so the PGWP isn't a dead end, but a runway.
