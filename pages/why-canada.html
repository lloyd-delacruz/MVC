By the numbers

A country built on arrival

Free at the point of care

Universal healthcare, since 1984.

Canada's publicly funded healthcare system means you don't choose between a doctor's visit and your rent. Permanent residents are covered by their province's plan — no premiums, no copays for hospital visits, no surprise bills. Whether you're starting a family, raising kids, or aging gracefully, healthcare is one less thing to worry about.

  • Hospital and physician services covered for residents
  • Prescription support for seniors and lower-income families
  • Mental-health programs expanding province by province
K–12 public schooling, free.

An education that opens doors.

Canadian public schools are tuition-free for permanent residents and regularly rank among the world's best. Three of the top fifty universities globally are Canadian — and graduates of Canadian programs unlock a Post-Graduation Work Permit that's the envy of international students everywhere.

  • Tuition-free public K–12 for residents
  • Three universities in the global top 50 (U of T, McGill, UBC)
  • Post-graduation work permits up to 3 years
Among the world's safest countries.

A place where everyday life is calm.

The Global Peace Index consistently places Canada in the world's top fifteen safest countries. Strong institutions, low violent-crime rates, and a culture of mutual respect mean kids walk to school, neighbours wave hello, and emergency rooms aren't filled with the consequences of gun violence.

  • Top 15 globally on the Global Peace Index
  • Strict firearm laws and low gun-related crime
  • Trust in police and judiciary among the highest in the OECD
A G7 economy hungry for talent.

Where your skills travel with you.

Canada is one of the few countries actively recruiting newcomers to fill a real labour gap. Tech corridors in Toronto, Vancouver, and Montreal are thriving. Healthcare, trades, transport, and engineering are short-staffed nationally — and credential-recognition programs are expanding province by province.

  • Express Entry processing in as little as 6 months
  • Open work permits for spouses of skilled workers
  • Federal minimum wage of $17.30/hour as of April 2024
Family is a federal priority.

Reunite. Stay close. Build something together.

Family-class sponsorship lets Canadian citizens and permanent residents bring spouses, children, parents, and grandparents to live with them permanently. Generous parental leave, the Canada Child Benefit, and affordable childcare in most provinces mean families don't just survive here — they grow.

  • Spousal sponsorship as fast as 12 months
  • Up to 18 months of paid parental leave
  • $10/day childcare rolling out nationally
200+ origin countries represented.

Multiculturalism — written into the constitution.

Canada is the only country in the world with multiculturalism enshrined in law. Your culture, your language, your faith — they aren't things to set aside at the border. They're things your neighbours come to celebrate. From Diwali on Parliament Hill to Lunar New Year in Vancouver, this country shows up.

  • Officially bilingual (English & French)
  • Most-spoken non-official languages: Mandarin, Punjabi, Spanish
  • Charter of Rights protects against religious & ethnic discrimination
48 national parks. 4 oceans.

A backyard the size of a continent.

The world's longest coastline. Forty-eight national parks. Glaciers, fjords, prairies, boreal forest, and the second-largest country on Earth holding only 0.5% of the world's population. There is room here — to breathe, to wander, to find your edge of nowhere.

  • 243,042 km of coastline — longest in the world
  • 9% of Earth's freshwater
  • UNESCO World Heritage sites from coast to coast
Charter-protected rights for all.

A democracy that takes rights seriously.

The Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms protects expression, religion, mobility, and equality — for citizens and permanent residents alike. The Economist consistently ranks Canada a "full democracy," ahead of every G7 peer. Press freedom, judicial independence, civil liberties: all top-tier.

  • Constitutional protection for religious & LGBTQ+ rights
  • Press Freedom Index: top 20 globally
  • Independent judiciary, accessible to all residents

A year in Canada

Four seasons. Four entirely different countries.

Yes, the winters are real. So are the wildflower hikes in May, the dock-jump summers, and the maple-leaf Octobers that look photoshopped. Pick a season.

March · April · May

The thaw, and the optimism that comes with it.

Snow melts into rivers, cherry blossoms blanket Vancouver and Toronto's High Park, and the country exhales. Sugar shacks open in Quebec. Hiking trails reopen in the Rockies. Birds you forgot about come back.

  • Cherry blossoms in Vancouver & Toronto
  • Maple-syrup season in rural Québec
  • Avg 8–18°C, longer days returning
June · July · August

The country lives outside.

Long northern daylight stretches past 10pm. Patios in Montréal stay open until last call. Cottagers head to the lakes. Calgary Stampede, Toronto's Caribbean Carnival, and a million backyard BBQs happen at once. Canada in summer is a different country entirely.

  • Festival season nationwide
  • Avg 18–28°C, sun until 10pm
  • National parks at peak access
September · October · November

The reason maple is on the flag.

For three weeks in October, the country turns red and gold. The Laurentians, the Niagara region, and Algonquin become the most photographed forests on earth. Thanksgiving's a weekend, not a day. Crisp mornings, warm cardigans — the most Canadian time to arrive.

  • Peak fall colours in October
  • Canadian Thanksgiving, second Monday of October
  • Avg 8–18°C, golden light
December · January · February

It's cold, yes. It's also the best part.

Skiing in Whistler. Skating on the Rideau Canal. Northern lights from Yellowknife. Hot tubs after toboggan runs. Yes, you'll buy a real winter coat — and then you'll find out that minus twenty in dry Canadian air feels nothing like the cold you imagine.

  • World-class skiing & skating
  • Northern lights season
  • Avg −15 to 0°C — but bright, dry, blue-sky days
