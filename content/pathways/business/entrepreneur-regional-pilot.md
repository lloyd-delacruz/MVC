# Overview
heading: "What is the Regional Pilot?"
content: |
  The Regional Pilot is the BC PNP Entrepreneur stream designed to channel new businesses into smaller communities outside Metro Vancouver. Where the Base Category has metro flexibility but higher thresholds, the Regional Pilot trades location flexibility for lower capital requirements — and a community that has actively endorsed your proposal.

  Participating communities are smaller centres located away from B.C.'s larger metros. Each community publishes its own priority economic-development sectors. Your business has to align with those priorities, and the community has to issue you a referral before you can register with the BC PNP.

  Once you have a community referral and the BC PNP invites you to apply, the path to permanent residence mirrors the Base Category: a temporary work permit support letter, time on the ground actually building the business, a Performance Agreement, and final nomination only after you've delivered. Because thresholds, participating communities, and allocations change from time to time, we confirm the current details with you before you commit.

# How It Works
heading: "How it works"
eyebrow: "The Process"
lede: "From shortlisting communities to permanent residence, here's what the journey looks like."
steps:
  - title: "Eligibility & community shortlist"
    description: "We confirm your net worth and ownership/management experience, then map your business idea against the priority sectors of participating communities, building a shortlist with the strongest fit."
  - title: "Exploratory visit"
    description: "You travel to the shortlisted community (or communities) to meet the designated economic-development contact, see the local market, and present your business proposal in person."
  - title: "Community referral"
    description: "If the community endorses your proposal, they issue a written referral. This is the gate — you cannot register without one."
  - title: "BC PNP registration"
    description: "We complete and submit your registration to the BC PNP: work experience, education, language, net worth, business proposal, and the community referral."
  - title: "Application & net-worth verification"
    description: "After invitation, you submit a full application. A BC PNP-authorized accounting firm verifies your net worth, legal source of funds, and accumulation history."
  - title: "Performance Agreement & work permit support"
    description: "On approval, you sign a Performance Agreement with the BC PNP and receive a work permit support letter, so you can move to the community and start operating."
  - title: "Establish & operate the business"
    description: "You arrive in the community, make your investment, set up operations, hire your Canadian or PR employee(s), and actively manage day-to-day operations until you've met the Performance Agreement milestones."
  - title: "Final nomination & PR"
    description: "Once you've delivered against the Performance Agreement, the BC PNP issues your provincial nomination. You submit your PR application to IRCC and complete medical, biometrics, and security checks. On approval, you become a Canadian permanent resident."

# FAQ
heading: "Regional Pilot FAQs"
eyebrow: "Frequently Asked"
items:
  - question: "Which B.C. communities participate in the Regional Pilot?"
    answer: "Participating communities have a population under 75,000 and are located at least 30 kilometres from a municipality of more than 75,000. Each enrolled community publishes its three priority economic-development sectors. The current list is maintained on WelcomeBC and changes over time as communities join or leave the pilot — we confirm the current list in your consultation."
  - question: "What is a community referral and how do I get one?"
    answer: "A community referral is a written endorsement from a participating community confirming that your business proposal aligns with their economic priorities. To get one, you contact the community's designated economic development contact, conduct an exploratory visit, present your business plan, and request a referral. You can only register with the BC PNP Regional Pilot after a community has issued you a referral."
  - question: "How does the Regional Pilot differ from the Base Category?"
    answer: "The Regional Pilot has lower thresholds — $300,000 net worth and $100,000 investment, versus $600,000 and $200,000 for Base Category. In exchange, you must locate your business in a participating community outside Metro Vancouver and obtain a community referral. The path to PR is otherwise the same."
  - question: "Can I start in a small community and move to Vancouver later?"
    answer: "Not before nomination. The whole point of the Regional Pilot is that you build a real business in the participating community. Moving before you receive your provincial nomination would put your Performance Agreement and nomination at risk. After you become a permanent resident, you can live anywhere in Canada — but the business commitment is real."
  - question: "Has anything changed recently with BC PNP allocations?"
    answer: "Yes. In January 2025 the federal government reduced British Columbia's annual PNP allocation from 8,000 to 4,000 — a 50% cut. The 2026–2028 federal Immigration Levels Plan announces planned increases to PNP admissions, but the Regional Pilot remains a competitive program. Strong community referrals and proposals matter more than ever."
