# Overview
heading: "What is the BC PNP Entrepreneur Base Category?"
content: |
  The Base Category is British Columbia's main entrepreneur stream. It is designed for experienced business owners and senior managers who want to start a new business or buy and grow an existing one in B.C. — anywhere in the province, including Metro Vancouver.

  Unlike federal investor programs, the Base Category is a two-step process: you arrive in B.C. on a 20-month work permit supported by the BC PNP, set up and actively manage your business, meet the performance milestones in your Performance Agreement, and only then receive your provincial nomination for permanent residence.

  Selection is competitive. After you submit a registration, the BC PNP scores it on human capital factors (work and ownership experience, education, official-language ability) and economic factors (commercial viability, jobs created, transferability to B.C., and benefit to the province). Registrations are ranked and the top-scoring proposals receive Invitations to Apply (ITAs).

# FAQ
heading: "Base Category FAQs"
eyebrow: "Frequently Asked"
items:
  - question: "What is the minimum personal net worth?"
    answer: "The minimum verified personal net worth is CAD $600,000. After invitation, a BC PNP-authorized accounting firm reviews your net worth, source of funds, and accumulation history before your nomination application can proceed."
  - question: "How much do I need to invest in my B.C. business?"
    answer: "The minimum eligible business investment is CAD $200,000. The investment must be in an eligible business that you will actively manage day-to-day, and you must create at least one new full-time job for a Canadian citizen or permanent resident."
  - question: "Do I get permanent residence right away?"
    answer: "No. The Base Category nominates you only after you arrive in B.C. and successfully establish your business. You first receive a BC PNP work permit support letter for a roughly 20-month work permit, then operate the business and meet your Performance Agreement milestones before final nomination and PR submission."
  - question: "Can the business be in Metro Vancouver?"
    answer: "Yes. Unlike the Regional Pilot, the Base Category permits businesses anywhere in British Columbia, including Metro Vancouver, Victoria, and other large metros."
  - question: "Has anything changed recently?"
    answer: "Yes. In January 2025 the federal government cut British Columbia's annual PNP allocation from 8,000 to 4,000 — a 50% reduction. The 2026–2028 Immigration Levels Plan signals increased PNP admissions over time, but allocations remain tighter than 2024 levels, so registration scoring and proposal quality are more important than ever."
  - question: "Should I choose Base Category or Regional Pilot?"
    answer: "The Regional Pilot has lower thresholds (net worth $300,000, investment $100,000) but restricts you to smaller communities outside Metro Vancouver and requires a community referral. Choose Base if you want metro flexibility and have the capital; choose Regional Pilot if your business fits a smaller community and your capital is between $300K and $600K."
