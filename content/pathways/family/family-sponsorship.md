# Overview
heading: "What is Family Sponsorship?"
content: |
  Canada's family class sponsorship lets a Canadian citizen or permanent resident
  sponsor close family members to become permanent residents. The most common
  categories are spouses, common-law and conjugal partners, dependent children,
  and parents and grandparents (under the Parents and Grandparents Program, with
  an annual intake cap).

  Sponsorship comes with a legal commitment called the **undertaking**.
  By signing it, you agree to provide for your sponsored family member's basic needs
  and to repay any social assistance they receive during the undertaking period —
  3 years for spouses and partners, up to 20 years for parents and grandparents.

  The biggest risks in sponsorship applications are not eligibility but presentation:
  relationship genuineness, document completeness, and financial proof. We help you
  build a file that anticipates IRCC's questions before they're asked.

# Do You Qualify
heading: "Do you qualify?"
content: |
  Sponsors and sponsored persons each need to meet specific requirements. Here are
  the most common.
items:
  - "**Sponsor is a Canadian citizen or permanent resident** aged 18 or older"
  - "Sponsor meets the **Minimum Necessary Income (MNI)** threshold for parents/grandparents (no income test for spouses or dependent children)"
  - "Sponsor is **not bankrupt**, not in default on a previous undertaking, and not in receipt of social assistance (except for disability)"
  - "The relationship is **genuine and not entered primarily for immigration purposes**"
  - "Sponsored person is **not inadmissible** on criminal, medical, or security grounds"
note: "Not sure if you meet the income or relationship requirements? [Book a free eligibility check](/pages/contact.html) — no obligation."

# How It Works
heading: "How it works"
lede: "From confirming eligibility to your family member's arrival in Canada."
steps:
  - title: "Confirm sponsor and sponsored eligibility"
    description: "We review the sponsor's status, income (where applicable), and the relationship category to confirm both parties qualify before any forms are filed."
  - title: "Gather documents"
    description: "Relationship evidence, financial records, ID and civil documents, photos, and affidavits. We give you a precise list and review every document for completeness."
  - title: "Submit the sponsorship application"
    description: "The sponsor and sponsored person applications are filed together to IRCC, with the undertaking signed and supporting evidence attached."
  - title: "Relationship assessment"
    description: "IRCC reviews the genuineness of the relationship. Additional evidence — or rarely an interview — may be requested."
  - title: "Medical & biometrics"
    description: "The sponsored person completes a medical exam with an IRCC-approved physician and provides biometrics at a Visa Application Centre."
  - title: "Decision & arrival"
    description: "Once approved, the sponsored person receives their Confirmation of Permanent Residence and (if required) a PR Visa, then completes landing in Canada."
