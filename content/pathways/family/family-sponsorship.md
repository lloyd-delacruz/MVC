# Overview
heading: "What is Family Sponsorship?"
content: |
  Canada's family class sponsorship lets a Canadian citizen or permanent resident sponsor close family members to become permanent residents. The most common categories are spouses, common-law and conjugal partners, dependent children, and parents and grandparents (the latter under the Parents and Grandparents Program, which has a limited annual intake).

  Sponsorship comes with a legal commitment called the **undertaking**. By signing it, you agree to provide for your sponsored family member's basic needs and to repay any social assistance they receive during the undertaking period. The length of that period depends on the category — shorter for spouses and partners, considerably longer for parents and grandparents.

  The biggest risks in sponsorship applications are usually not eligibility but presentation: relationship genuineness, document completeness, and financial proof. We help you build a file that anticipates IRCC's questions before they're asked.

# Do You Qualify
heading: "Do you qualify?"
content: |
  Sponsors and sponsored persons each need to meet specific requirements. These are the most common, but exact criteria are set by IRCC and can change, so treat this as a starting point rather than a final checklist.
items:
  - "The sponsor is a Canadian citizen or permanent resident, aged 18 or older"
  - "The sponsor meets the income threshold that applies to their category (an income test applies to parent and grandparent sponsorships; there is generally no income test for spouses or dependent children)"
  - "The sponsor is not bankrupt, not in default on a previous undertaking, and not receiving social assistance (with a limited exception for disability)"
  - "The relationship is genuine and not entered into primarily for immigration purposes"
  - "The sponsored person is not inadmissible on criminal, medical, or security grounds"
