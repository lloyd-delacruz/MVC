# Overview
heading: "What is Express Entry?"
content: |
  Express Entry is the Government of Canada's online system for managing applications
  from skilled workers who want to become permanent residents. It manages three federal
  economic programs: the **Federal Skilled Worker Program (FSW)**, the
  **Canadian Experience Class (CEC)**, and the
  **Federal Skilled Trades Program (FST)**.

  When you create a profile, Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada
  (**IRCC**) ranks you against other candidates using the Comprehensive
  Ranking System (**CRS**). The CRS scores you out of 1,200 based on age,
  education, language ability, work experience, and adaptability factors. Roughly every
  two weeks, IRCC issues Invitations to Apply (**ITA**) to the highest-scoring
  candidates in a draw.

  This is where good representation matters most. Your CRS score isn't fixed — it can
  often be improved by retaking language tests, securing a provincial nomination, or
  submitting documents in the right order. We assess your profile and identify the
  highest-impact moves for your specific situation.

# Do You Qualify
heading: "Do you qualify?"
content: |
  These are the core requirements for most Express Entry candidates. Your specific
  situation may have additional considerations.
items:
  - "Language test results at **CLB 7 or higher** in all four abilities (reading, writing, speaking, listening)"
  - "At least **1 year of skilled work experience** (NOC TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3) within the last 10 years"
  - "Valid **Educational Credential Assessment (ECA)** for foreign education"
  - "Sufficient **settlement funds** (unless you have a valid Canadian job offer or are already working in Canada)"
  - "No **criminal or medical inadmissibility** to Canada"
  - "**CRS score competitive** with recent draws (typically 470+ for all-program draws, lower for category-based draws)"
note: "Not sure if you qualify? [Book a free eligibility check](/pages/contact.html) — no obligation."

# How It Works
heading: "How it works"
lede: "From profile creation to PR confirmation, here's what the journey looks like."
steps:
  - title: "Create your Express Entry profile"
    description: "We assemble your language results, ECA, work experience proof, and personal details into a complete IRCC profile — accurate, complete, and optimized for CRS points."
  - title: "Enter the Express Entry pool"
    description: "Your profile sits in the pool with a CRS score and a unique identifier. Profiles stay in the pool for up to 12 months. We monitor every draw and update your profile as new credentials, language scores, or job offers come in."
  - title: "Receive an Invitation to Apply (ITA)"
    description: "When IRCC's draw cut-off meets or falls below your score, you receive an ITA. You then have 60 days to submit your full PR application."
  - title: "Submit your PR application"
    description: "We prepare and file the full electronic application package — police certificates, medical, reference letters, ECA, language results, and all required forms."
  - title: "Biometrics & medical"
    description: "IRCC requests biometrics (fingerprints and photo) at a local Visa Application Centre. The medical exam is completed with an IRCC-approved panel physician."
  - title: "Decision & Confirmation of PR"
    description: "Once approved, you receive your Confirmation of Permanent Residence (COPR) and PR Visa (if applicable). You then complete landing — either at a port of entry or via the online landing process."
