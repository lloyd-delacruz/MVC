# Overview
heading: "What is the Provincial Nominee Program?"
content: |
  The Provincial Nominee Program (**PNP**) lets each of Canada's
  13 provinces and territories nominate immigrants who meet local labour-market
  and demographic needs. Every province runs its own streams — Ontario's
  **OINP**, British Columbia's **BC PNP**, Alberta's
  **AINP**, Saskatchewan's **SINP**, and so on.

  Streams generally fall into two types. **Base streams** are
  stand-alone provincial nominations that lead to PR through a paper-based
  application. **Enhanced streams** are aligned with Express Entry —
  a provincial nomination through an enhanced stream adds 600 CRS points —
  in practice, well above any recent Express Entry cutoff.

  Each stream has its own eligibility criteria — some target specific occupations,
  some require a job offer, some prioritize ties to the province through study or
  family. Picking the right province and stream is the single most important
  decision in the PNP process. We help match your profile to the streams with the
  best chance of success.

# Do You Qualify
eyebrow: "Eligibility"
heading: "Do you qualify?"
content: |
  PNP eligibility varies dramatically by province and stream — these are the most
  common requirements you'll need to meet.
items:
  - "A **job offer** from a provincial employer, or a strong **connection to the province** (study, work, family)"
  - "Language scores at the level required by your target stream (CLB 4–7 typical)"
  - "Relevant **work or study experience** — most streams require either skilled work history or Canadian education"
  - "Demonstrated **intent to settle and work in the nominating province**"
  - "Stream-specific criteria (occupation lists, NOC TEER level, education, age, ties)"
  - "No criminal or medical inadmissibility to Canada"

# How It Works

  - title: "Check stream eligibility"

  - title: "Submit Expression of Interest (EOI)"

  - title: "Apply for PR (or work permit)"
    description: "With your nomination, you apply to IRCC for permanent residence — through Express Entry (enhanced streams) or via a paper-based application (base streams). Some streams allow a temporary work permit while your PR is processed."
