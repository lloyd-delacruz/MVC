# Overview
heading: "What is the Provincial Nominee Program?"
content: |
  The Provincial Nominee Program (**PNP**) lets participating provinces and
  territories nominate immigrants who meet their local labour-market and
  demographic needs. Each participating province runs its own streams — Ontario,
  British Columbia, Alberta, Saskatchewan, and others each operate their own
  programs with distinct rules.

  Streams generally fall into two types. **Base streams** are
  stand-alone provincial nominations that lead to PR through a paper-based
  application. **Enhanced streams** are aligned with Express Entry —
  a provincial nomination through an enhanced stream adds a substantial number of
  CRS points, in practice enough to sit well above recent Express Entry cut-offs.

  Each stream has its own eligibility criteria — some target specific occupations,
  some require a job offer, some prioritize ties to the province through study or
  family. Picking the right province and stream is the single most important
  decision in the PNP process. Because streams, occupation lists, and point
  thresholds change often, we help match your profile to the options with the
  best chance of success at the time you apply.

# Do You Qualify
eyebrow: "Eligibility"
heading: "Do you qualify?"
content: |
  PNP eligibility varies dramatically by province and stream. These are the most
  common building blocks, but exact criteria are set by each province and change
  regularly, so treat this as a starting point rather than a final checklist.
items:
  - "A **job offer** from a provincial employer, or a strong **connection to the province** (study, work, or family)"
  - "Language scores at the level required by your target stream"
  - "Relevant **work or study experience** — most streams require either skilled work history or Canadian education"
  - "Demonstrated **intent to settle and work in the nominating province**"
  - "Stream-specific criteria (occupation lists, NOC TEER level, education, age, ties)"
  - "No criminal or medical inadmissibility to Canada"

# How It Works

    description: "With your nomination, you apply to IRCC for permanent residence, through Express Entry (enhanced streams) or a paper-based application (base streams). Some streams allow a temporary work permit while your PR is processed."
