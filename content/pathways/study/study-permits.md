# Overview
heading: "What is a Canadian Study Permit?"
content: |
  A study permit is the document that lets you study at a
  **Designated Learning Institution (DLI)** in Canada — colleges,
  universities, language schools, and trade schools approved by the provincial or
  territorial government. The institution must be on the official DLI list, and
  most programs over 6 months require a study permit.

  Most applicants choose between two streams. The
  **Student Direct Stream (SDS)** is faster — typically 8–12 weeks
  — but requires upfront proof of funds via a Guaranteed Investment Certificate,
  tuition payment, and stronger language scores. The **regular stream**
  is open to everyone but takes longer and requires more comprehensive documentation.

  For many international students, the bigger picture is the
  **Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)**. After completing an eligible
  program of at least 8 months, you can work in Canada for up to 3 years — and
  that Canadian work experience is one of the strongest pathways to permanent
  residence through Express Entry's Canadian Experience Class.

# Do You Qualify
heading: "Do you qualify?"
content: |
  Most refusals come down to weak ties or insufficient funds. Here's what IRCC
  looks for.
items:
  - "A **Letter of Acceptance from a DLI** for an eligible program"
  - "**Proof of funds** — tuition + living expenses + travel for the first year (and beyond, depending on program length)"
  - "No **criminal inadmissibility** — and a medical exam if required"
  - "Demonstrated **strong ties to your home country**, or a clear and credible plan for a PGWP-led pathway to PR"
  - "A genuine, well-articulated **study plan** showing how this program fits your career goals"
