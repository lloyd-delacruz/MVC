# Overview
heading: "What is a Visitor Visa?"
content: |
  A **Temporary Resident Visa (TRV)** — commonly called a visitor visa
  or tourist visa — is the document most foreign nationals need to enter Canada
  for short stays. Whether you need one depends primarily on your passport.
  Citizens of visa-required countries need a TRV; citizens of visa-exempt countries
  travelling by air need an
  **Electronic Travel Authorization (eTA)** instead.

  TRVs come in single-entry and multiple-entry forms. Multiple-entry visas are
  issued in most cases and let you visit Canada repeatedly during the visa's
  validity. Visitors already in Canada can also apply to extend their stay
  through a **visitor record**.

  The most common refusal reasons are weak ties to the home country, insufficient
  funds, and an unclear purpose of visit. A well-organized application that
  addresses these concerns directly — with the right supporting letters —
  significantly improves your odds.

# Do You Qualify
eyebrow: "Eligibility"
heading: "Do you qualify?"
content: |
  IRCC's job is to be satisfied that you'll come, visit as planned, and leave on
  time. These are the elements they assess. Exact requirements can change, so
  treat this as a starting point rather than a final checklist.
items:
  - "A **valid passport** covering the entire duration of your intended stay"
  - "**Proof of funds** sufficient for your visit and return travel"
  - "Demonstrated **strong ties to your home country** — employment, property, family, or other reasons to return"
  - "No **criminal or medical inadmissibility** to Canada"
  - "A **genuine visitor intent** — you're not coming to work, study, or stay permanently"
  - "A clearly **documented purpose of visit** — itinerary, invitation letter, or business agenda"
note: "Have you been refused before, or worried about your ties? Book a free eligibility check — no obligation."
