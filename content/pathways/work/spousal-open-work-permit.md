# Overview
heading: "What is a Spousal Open Work Permit?"
content: |
  For couples that are **married or in a common-law relationship**,
  the Spousal Open Work Permit allows the spouse of an international student —
  or of a PGWP holder working in a skilled position — to come to Canada and
  work for almost any employer, full-time or part-time, anywhere in the country.

  "Open" is the key word. Your partner is not tied to a specific employer, role,
  or province. They can change jobs freely, work in any sector, and start
  accumulating Canadian work experience that strengthens the family's case for
  permanent residence later on.

  For many couples, this permit is what makes the move to Canada possible —
  financially, emotionally, and practically. One person's study journey becomes
  two careers, two incomes, and a shared route to PR.

# Key Facts

# Do You Qualify
eyebrow: "Eligibility"
heading: "Does your spouse qualify?"
content: |
  Eligibility depends on what *you* are doing in Canada — studying, or
  working under a PGWP. Either route opens the door for your partner.
groups:
  - heading: "If you are studying full-time"
    items:
      - "You hold a **valid study permit**"
      - "You are a **full-time student**"
      - "You are studying at a **Designated Learning Institution that offers PGWP-eligible programs**"
  - heading: "If you are working under a PGWP"
    items:
      - "You hold a **valid work permit**"
      - "You are working in a **skilled position** (TEER 0, 1, 2, or 3)"
      - "Your work permit has **at least 6 months remaining** when your spouse applies"
note: "Rules around eligible programs and skill levels change. We confirm both partners' status before filing. Send us your details for a quick assessment."
