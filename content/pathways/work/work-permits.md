# Overview
heading: "What is an LMIA?"
content: |
  A Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA) is a document from Employment and Social Development Canada (ESDC) that confirms hiring a foreign worker won't displace a Canadian worker. It's obtained by the employer, not the worker, and for many job-offer-based work permits it's the first domino — your permit application depends on it. A complete, well-prepared LMIA is often the difference between a smooth approval and months of delay or a refusal, which is why we coordinate closely with the employer on their side of the file.

  Not every work permit needs one, though. Many are LMIA-exempt through international agreements and government policies: the International Experience Canada (IEC) youth mobility program, CUSMA (formerly NAFTA) for eligible U.S. and Mexican professionals, the Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) for international graduates, and intra-company transfers, among others.

  ## What is a Canadian Work Permit?

  A Canadian work permit lets a foreign national work in Canada, either for a specific employer or, in some cases, for almost any employer. There are two broad categories. Closed (employer-specific) work permits are tied to a single named employer, role, and location, and often require an LMIA. Open work permits let you work for almost any employer anywhere in Canada and are reserved for specific situations, such as spouses of certain workers or students and post-graduation graduates.

  Choosing the right category — and getting the documentation right — is the single biggest factor in how fast your application moves and whether it's approved.

# Do You Qualify
heading: "Do you qualify?"
content: |
  Specifics vary widely by category. These are the requirements common to most work permits, but exact criteria change and depend on the category, so treat this as a starting point rather than a final checklist.
items:
  - "A **valid job offer** from a Canadian employer, or eligibility under an LMIA-exempt or open category"
  - "No **criminal or medical inadmissibility** to Canada"
  - "**Sufficient funds** to support yourself (and any accompanying family) during your stay"
  - "Demonstrated **intent to leave at the end of your authorized stay**, or clear eligibility to extend or transition to PR"
  - "Meeting the **occupation requirements** for the role — qualifications, licensing, and any language needs"
