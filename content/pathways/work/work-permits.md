# Overview
heading: "What is a Canadian Work Permit?"
content: |
  A Canadian work permit lets a foreign national work for a specific employer or
  (in some cases) any employer in Canada. There are two broad categories.
  **Closed work permits** are tied to a single named employer, role,
  and location. **Open work permits** let you work for almost any
  employer anywhere in Canada and are reserved for specific situations like
  spouses of skilled workers and post-graduation graduates.

  Closed permits typically require a
  **Labour Market Impact Assessment (LMIA)** — a document from
  Employment and Social Development Canada confirming that hiring a foreign
  worker won't displace a Canadian. But many work permits are
  **LMIA-exempt** through international agreements and government
  policies: the **International Experience Canada (IEC)** youth
  mobility program, **CUSMA** (formerly NAFTA) for U.S. and
  Mexican professionals, the **Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)**
  for international graduates, and intra-company transfers.

  Choosing the right category — and getting the documentation right — is the
  difference between a 4-week approval and months of processing or a refusal.

# Do You Qualify
heading: "Do you qualify?"
content: |
  Specifics vary widely by category. These are the common requirements across
  most work permits.
items:
  - "A **valid job offer** from a Canadian employer or eligibility under an LMIA-exempt category"
  - "No **criminal or medical inadmissibility** to Canada"
  - "**Sufficient funds** to support yourself (and any accompanying family) during your stay"
  - "Demonstrated **intent to leave at the end of your authorized stay**, or clear eligibility to extend or transition to PR"
  - "You meet the **occupation requirements** — qualifications, licensing, language for the role"
