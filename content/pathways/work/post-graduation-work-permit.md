# Overview
heading: "What is a Post-Graduation Work Permit?"
content: |
  A **Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)** is an open work permit
  that lets graduates of eligible Canadian programs stay and work in Canada
  after they finish their studies. It's open — meaning you can work for almost
  any employer, in almost any role, anywhere in Canada.

  For most international students, the PGWP is the bridge that turns a study
  permit into **permanent residence**. Twelve months of skilled
  Canadian work experience opens the door to Express Entry's Canadian
  Experience Class — and provincial streams designed to retain graduates
  educated here.

  The length of your PGWP depends on the length of the program you completed.
  Generally: 8–24 months of study yields a PGWP equal to your program length;
  a 2-year-or-longer program can yield a 3-year PGWP. We'll map this out for
  you precisely based on your DLI and program.

# Do You Qualify
eyebrow: "Eligibility"
heading: "Do you qualify?"
content: |
  The PGWP rewards completion of a real, full-time program at a recognised
  institution. The criteria are firm — but clear.
items:
  - "You are **18 years of age or older**"
  - "You held a **valid study permit** for the duration of your studies"
  - "You graduated from a **PGWP-eligible Designated Learning Institution**"
  - "You completed a **full-time program of at least 8 months** in length"
  - "You apply within **180 days** of receiving final confirmation that you completed the program"
note: "Unsure if your DLI or program qualifies? Send us your transcript — we'll confirm eligibility before you apply."
