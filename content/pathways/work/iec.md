# Overview
heading: "What is International Experience Canada?"
content: |
  International Experience Canada — IEC — is a set of youth mobility agreements between Canada and partner countries that lets young citizens travel and work in Canada temporarily, with paperwork far simpler than a standard work permit.

  IEC is open to citizens of roughly 35 partner countries, usually aged 18 to 30 or 18 to 35 depending on which country you hold a passport from. There is no LMIA, no employer petition, and no PR commitment — but it can absolutely lead there.

  For many people, IEC is the cleanest first chapter in a longer Canadian story: spend a year working here, build experience, decide if Canada is home, and then transition into a PGWP-equivalent or Express Entry path.

# Do You Qualify
heading: "Do you qualify?"
eyebrow: "Eligibility"
lede: "Eligibility starts with your passport — only citizens of partner countries can apply. From there, it's about age, funds, and the right stream."
items:
  - "Citizenship of an IEC partner country (passport-based — permanent residents do not qualify)"
  - "Age 18 to 30 or 35 at the time of application — exact bracket depends on country"
  - "Proof of funds — typically CAD 2,500 — and a return ticket or the funds to buy one"
  - "Health insurance for the entire duration of your stay"
  - "No criminal inadmissibility — and a medical exam if your work involves children, healthcare, or agriculture"
  - "For Young Professionals and Co-op: a signed Canadian offer letter meeting stream criteria"
note: "IEC is pool-based — you create a profile, then wait to be invited. Quotas are competitive in some countries. Talk to us early to time your profile against the season."

# How It Works
heading: "How it works"

lede: "From profile creation to landing in Canada — the IEC route is faster than most work-permit pathways, but only if every step is timed right."
steps:
  - title: "Create an IEC profile"
    description: "You build a profile with the IRCC pool, tagged for your country, age, and chosen stream. Your country's pool opens annually with a fixed quota."
  - title: "Receive an invitation to apply"
    description: "Invitations are issued in rounds throughout the season. We watch the draws and prepare your file in advance, so you can submit in the tight 10-day window once invited."
  - title: "Submit the work permit application"
    description: "Filed online with your passport, photos, police certificate, proof of funds, insurance, and (for Young Pro / Co-op) the qualifying offer letter or co-op agreement."
  - title: "Biometrics and medical (if required)"
    description: "Provided at your local Visa Application Centre. Medicals are required only for some occupations and certain countries."
  - title: "Receive the Port of Entry letter"
    description: "Approval comes as a Port of Entry letter, valid for 12 months. Your physical work permit is issued by a CBSA officer when you actually arrive."
  - title: "Plan the next chapter"
    description: "IEC is a one-time-per-stream entitlement for most countries. We help you pivot — into a PGWP-style program, an LMIA-supported permit, or directly into Express Entry — before the IEC permit runs out."
