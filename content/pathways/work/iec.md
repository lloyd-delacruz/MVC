# Overview
heading: "What is International Experience Canada?"
content: |
  International Experience Canada (IEC) is a set of youth-mobility agreements between Canada and partner countries that lets young citizens travel and work in Canada temporarily, with paperwork far simpler than a standard work permit.

  IEC is open to citizens of Canada's partner countries, usually within an age range that depends on which country's passport you hold. There is no LMIA, no employer petition, and no PR commitment — but it can absolutely lead there.

  For many people, IEC is the cleanest first chapter in a longer Canadian story: spend time working here, build experience, decide if Canada is home, and then transition into a further work permit or an Express Entry path.

# Do You Qualify
heading: "Do you qualify?"
eyebrow: "Eligibility"
lede: "Eligibility starts with your passport — only citizens of partner countries can apply. From there, it's about age, funds, and the right stream. Exact criteria are set by IRCC and each bilateral agreement and can change, so treat this as a starting point rather than a final checklist."
items:
  - "Citizenship of an IEC partner country (passport-based — permanent residents do not qualify)"
  - "Age within your country's eligible bracket at the time of application"
  - "Proof of funds to support your arrival, plus a return ticket or the means to buy one"
  - "Health insurance for the entire duration of your stay"
  - "No criminal inadmissibility — and a medical exam if your work involves children, healthcare, or agriculture"
  - "For Young Professionals and Co-op: a signed Canadian offer letter or co-op agreement meeting stream criteria"
note: "IEC is pool-based — you create a profile, then wait to be invited. Quotas are competitive in some countries. Talk to us early to time your profile against the season."

# How It Works
heading: "How it works"
eyebrow: "The Process"
lede: "From profile creation to landing in Canada, here's the path. The IEC route is faster than most work-permit pathways, but only if every step is timed right."
steps:
  - title: "Create an IEC profile"
    description: "You build a profile in the IRCC pool, tagged for your country, age, and chosen stream. Each country's pool opens on its own schedule, with a set quota."
  - title: "Receive an invitation to apply"
    description: "Invitations are issued in rounds throughout the season. We watch the draws and prepare your file in advance, so you can submit within the tight window once invited."
  - title: "Submit the work permit application"
    description: "Filed online with your passport, photos, police certificate, proof of funds, insurance, and (for Young Professionals or Co-op) the qualifying offer letter or co-op agreement."
  - title: "Biometrics and medical (if required)"
    description: "Provided at your local Visa Application Centre. Medicals are required only for some occupations and certain countries."
  - title: "Receive the Port of Entry letter"
    description: "Approval comes as a Port of Entry letter. Your physical work permit is issued by a CBSA officer when you arrive."
  - title: "Plan the next chapter"
    description: "IEC is a one-time-per-stream entitlement for most countries. We help you pivot — into a further work permit, an LMIA-supported role, or directly into Express Entry — before the IEC permit runs out."
